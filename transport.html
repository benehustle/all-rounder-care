NDIS transport that stays on schedule

Reliable transport helps participants attend therapy, education, work experience, and family events without relying on informal networks every time. All Rounder Care provides funded travel across Perth — South of the River where your plan includes transport or activity-based supports that cover worker time behind the wheel.

We confirm pick-up windows the day before, allow extra minutes for hoist transfers, and keep families updated if traffic affects the return leg.

What is involved

We map routes that suit your mobility equipment, plan rest stops on longer runs to Mandurah or inner Perth, and secure wheelchairs to Australian Design Rule expectations for the vehicle class being used. Workers hold current driver licences for the relevant class and follow road rules set out in WA traffic regulations.

Mileage, tolls, and parking are discussed up front so invoices match the NDIS guidance that applies to your funding line.

What to expect during a trip

We arrive within the agreed window, help you reach the vehicle safely, and check seatbelts or harnesses before moving. Music, conversation, or a quiet ride — your preference leads. At drop-off we hand over to the receiving party you nominate or wait until your appointment finishes if that is funded.

If a vehicle fault appears during the shift, we organise backup transport and let you know who will cover any extra cost under your service agreement.

Three clear benefits

  • Predictable communication when school bells or clinic slots are tight.
  • Vehicles chosen to match mobility and sensory needs where possible.
  • Local drivers who know peak pinch points on the Kwinana Freeway corridor.

Questions families often ask

Does NDIS transport cover fuel for family-owned vehicles?

Some plans include funding for activity-based transport or reimbursements in line with NDIA guidance. We explain the options that apply to your plan and refer complex claims to your plan manager.

Can two participants share a trip to the same program?

Shared transport is possible when each participant’s plan allows it and privacy is respected. We obtain written agreement from both households and adjust invoicing so costs stay transparent.

What car seats or boosters are used for children?

We follow Western Australian child restraint laws, use approved seats matched to age and size, and install them to manufacturer instructions before the first trip.
