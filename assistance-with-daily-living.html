How we support daily living

Daily living supports under the NDIS are meant to build independence where you want it and reliable help where you need it. At All Rounder Care we work with participants and families across Perth — South of the River to map out cleaning, meal preparation, laundry, and general household organisation in a way that matches your plan and your routines.

Before we start, we talk through allergies, equipment, pets, access paths, and any cultural preferences so visits feel predictable. Our workers arrive with a clear task list agreed with you, and we adjust as seasons, health, or funding lines change.

What is involved

Typical shifts can include vacuuming, bathroom and kitchen cleaning, changing linen, grocery shopping with or for you, batch cooking, and labelling meals for the week ahead. We can also help sort paperwork, set medication reminders alongside your pharmacist or nurse advice, and prepare the home before allied health visits.

If you share a property with others, we coordinate tasks so everyone knows what is being handled during the rostered window. Where a task requires a registered trade — for example electrical repairs — we flag it early and help you organise the right licensed professional under separate arrangements.

What to expect on a shift

Workers introduce themselves at the door, confirm the plan for the day, and check if anything has changed since the last visit. We wear identifiable clothing, bring basic cleaning supplies unless you prefer your own products, and record notes you request for plan reviews.

We keep an eye on trip hazards, smoke alarms you can reach without a ladder, and hot surfaces in the kitchen, and we speak up if we notice something that might need a follow-up with your support coordinator.

Three clear benefits

  • Routines built around your preferences, not a generic checklist.
  • Communication that stays plain and direct for families and coordinators.
  • Flexibility when your goals shift between independence and higher assistance.

Questions families often ask

Can NDIS daily living supports include meal prep for special diets?

Yes, when your plan funding and goals align. We follow instructions from your dietitian or GP where you choose to share them, label containers clearly, and store food to Food Standards Australia New Zealand guidance for home kitchens.

How do workers meet WA screening rules before entering my home?

NDIS providers in Western Australia must ensure workers hold valid NDIS Worker Screening or acceptable equivalent checks where the rules allow. We keep records ready for audit and only roster screened workers to participant shifts.

What if I need to cancel or shorten a shift around a hospital visit?

Call us as soon as you know dates change. We will reschedule where possible, update your service agreement notes, and talk honestly about any notice periods set out in your agreement so there are no surprises.
